# Sanity Management Guide – Stay Sane, Stay Alive

Sanity is your most important resource in Animal Hospital. When it drops too low, your vision distorts, anomalies become harder to detect, and you become more vulnerable to supernatural threats. At zero Sanity, the game is effectively over.

This guide covers everything you need to know about **managing, protecting, and recovering Sanity** throughout your shift.

---

## What Drains Sanity

| Source | Sanity Cost | Avoidability |
|---|---|---|
| **Cursed Photo** (picking it up) | -10 | Avoidable — inspect on desk before touching |
| **Camera Anomaly / Demon Cat** (jumpscare) | -20 | Partially avoidable — press Exit quickly |
| **The Stalker** (eye contact) | -10 per stare | Avoidable — don't look at it |
| **Unchecked anomalies** | Varies | Avoidable — follow the 3-step protocol |
| **Environmental hazards** | Varies | Partially avoidable — use counters (Eye Drops, etc.) |
| **General exposure** | Slow drain over time | Inevitable — plan for recovery |

## How to Recover Sanity

### Items

| Item | Sanity per Use | Uses | Total Recovery | How to Get |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Coffee** | +5 per sip | 3 sips | +15 total | Found in break rooms, offices |
| **Chocolate** | +20 per bite | 3 bites | +60 total | Found in supply rooms, desks |

#### Coffee

- **+5 Sanity per sip**, up to **3 sips** per coffee item.
- **Total: +15 Sanity** per coffee.
- Best used as a **quick top-up between patients** or when you need a small Sanity boost to stay functional.
- Plentiful and easy to find — grab coffee whenever you pass a break room.

#### Chocolate

- **+20 Sanity per bite**, up to **3 bites** per chocolate item.
- **Total: +60 Sanity** per chocolate — the best single Sanity recovery item in the game.
- Save Chocolate for **emergencies or major Sanity drops** (e.g., after a Cursed Photo or Stalker encounter).
- More rare than Coffee — don't waste it on small dips.

### Class-Based Recovery

Certain staff roles have **built-in Sanity recovery mechanics** that make them ideal for long shifts.

| Class | Sanity Recovery Method | Effectiveness |
|---|---|---|
| **Doctor** | Heal patients | Recover Sanity as part of normal duties |
| **Surgeon** | Complete surgeries | Recover Sanity through successful operations |
| **Secretary** | Perform check-ins | Recover Sanity during administrative tasks |
| **Psychologist** | Special Sanity abilities | Dedicated Sanity recovery tools |

#### Doctor

Doctors recover Sanity naturally by **healing patients**. Every successful treatment restores a portion of Sanity, making the Doctor class the best choice for players who want Sanity sustain without relying on items.

- **Pros:** Passive Sanity recovery during normal gameplay
- **Cons:** Must successfully treat patients to benefit

#### Surgeon

Surgeons recover Sanity by **completing surgeries**. This ties Sanity recovery to one of the highest-stakes activities in the game — but the reward is proportional to the risk.

- **Pros:** Large Sanity recovery per successful surgery
- **Cons:** Surgeries are time-pressured (Tendril hazard) and risky

#### Secretary

Secretaries recover Sanity through **patient check-ins** — the administrative process of admitting patients to the hospital. This is the most consistent and lowest-risk Sanity recovery method.

- **Pros:** Reliable, low-risk, frequent opportunities
- **Cons:** Smaller per-action recovery compared to Doctor/Surgeon

#### Psychologist

The Psychologist has **dedicated Sanity recovery abilities** that no other class possesses. This makes the Psychologist the specialist choice for Sanity management.

- **Pros:** Most powerful Sanity toolkit in the game
- **Cons:** May have reduced effectiveness in other medical tasks

## Best Practices

### Prevention > Recovery

The best way to manage Sanity is to **avoid losing it in the first place**:

1. **Always inspect photos on the desk before picking them up** — prevents Cursed Photo loss (-10).
2. **Press Exit immediately when you see a Camera Anomaly** — prevents Demon Cat jumpscare (-20).
3. **Never look at The Stalker** — prevents stare Sanity loss (-10).
4. **Follow the 3-step screening protocol** — catching anomalies early prevents ongoing Sanity drain.
5. **Use Eye Drops for Don't Look Up** — prevents ceiling Sanity drain.

### Item Management

- **Carry at least 1 Chocolate and 1 Coffee** at all times.
- **Use Coffee for small dips** (under -10 Sanity lost).
- **Save Chocolate for big hits** (Cursed Photo, Camera Anomaly, Stalker).
- **Restock items when passing supply rooms** — don't wait until you're desperate.

### Class Selection

Choose your class based on your playstyle and experience level:

| Experience Level | Recommended Class | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Beginner** | Secretary | Consistent, low-risk Sanity recovery |
| **Intermediate** | Doctor | Passive recovery through normal gameplay |
| **Advanced** | Surgeon | High reward for skilled players |
| **Sanity-focused** | Psychologist | Dedicated Sanity toolkit |

## Advanced Tactics

### The Sanity Buffer

Never let your Sanity drop below **30%** if possible. Below this threshold:

- Visual distortion makes anomaly detection significantly harder.
- You're more vulnerable to monster encounters.
- Recovery items are less efficient (you need more of them).

Keep a **"panic buffer" of 30+ Sanity** at all times using Coffee sips between patients.

### Shift Planning

Plan your item usage around your **shift timeline**:

- **Early shift:** Focus on prevention. Inspect everything carefully. Don't waste items.
- **Mid shift:** Start using Coffee for maintenance. Begin stacking Chocolate reserves.
- **Late shift:** Anomaly frequency increases. Use Chocolate aggressively. Prioritize survival over thorough screening.

### The Secretary Loop

If playing Secretary, maximize Sanity recovery by:

1. Admitting patients rapidly (frequent check-ins = frequent recovery).
2. Staying near the reception area (close to Coffee and Chocolate supplies).
3. Letting Doctors/Surgeons handle the dangerous anomalies while you maintain Sanity.

### Emergency Recovery

If your Sanity drops dangerously low:

1. **Pop a Chocolate immediately** (+20 per bite, up to +60).
2. **Avoid all Sanity threats** — don't look at cameras, don't pick up photos, don't engage monsters.
3. **Find a safe room** and wait for a brief respite.
4. **Use Coffee to stabilize** while Chocolate recharges (they're separate items).
5. **Play Secretary or Psychologist** if you can switch roles — their passive recovery helps you climb back.

## Sanity Recovery Quick Reference

| Situation | Best Response |
|---|---|
| Small dip (0–10 lost) | Coffee sip (+5) |
| Medium dip (10–20 lost) | Coffee (2 sips, +10) or Chocolate bite (+20) |
| Large hit (20–30 lost) | Chocolate bite (+20) + Coffee sip (+5) |
| Critical (< 30% Sanity) | Full Chocolate (+60 if all 3 bites available) + avoid threats |
| After Camera Anomaly (-20) | Chocolate bite (+20) — immediate full recovery from this source |
| After Cursed Photo (-10) | 2 Coffee sips (+10) — saves Chocolate for bigger threats |

## Summary

Sanity management is a **resource game within the game**. The players who survive the longest are the ones who:

1. **Prevent Sanity loss** through careful play (inspecting photos, avoiding Stalker eye contact, pressing Exit on Demon Cat).
2. **Use items wisely** (Coffee for small dips, Chocolate for emergencies).
3. **Choose the right class** for their skill level and playstyle.
4. **Maintain a Sanity buffer** of 30%+ at all times.
5. **Adapt their strategy** as the shift progresses and anomaly frequency increases.

Stay sane. Stay alive.
