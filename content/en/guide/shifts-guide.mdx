# Animal Hospital All Shifts Guide — Shift System & Difficulty Explained

The shift system is the backbone of Animal Hospital's progression. Every round you play is a "shift" — a timed session where you screen animal patients, identify anomalies, and try to survive until the clock runs out. This guide breaks down **how shifts work**, **how many there are**, **how difficulty escalates**, and **how to survive the toughest ones**.

---

## What Is a Shift?

A **shift** is one complete round of Animal Hospital. It has a fixed duration (typically 5–8 minutes), during which:

1. **Patients arrive** at the reception window — you must screen each one.
2. **Anomalies appear** disguised as patients — you must identify and reject them.
3. **Monsters roam** the hospital — you must avoid or deal with them.
4. **Sanity drains** — you must manage it using items and class abilities.
5. **The timer runs out** — surviving until the end means completing the shift.

If your Sanity hits 0 during a shift, you are **eliminated** and that shift does not count as completed.

---

## How Many Shifts Are There?

Based on community testing and player reports, Animal Hospital features approximately **15–20 shifts** before the ending triggers. The exact count can vary due to a few factors:

| Factor | Impact on Total Shifts |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Anomaly identification rate** | Catching more anomalies per shift may reduce the total count needed |
| **Game updates** | The developer may adjust shift counts in patches |
| **Difficulty modifiers** | Some server configurations may affect progression speed |

Most players report reaching the ending around **shift 15–18**. The shift system is designed to gradually increase in difficulty, so the final shifts are significantly harder than the first.

## How Difficulty Scales Across Shifts

Difficulty in Animal Hospital scales along **three main axes**:

### 1. Anomaly Frequency

Early shifts feature relatively few anomalies — perhaps 1–2 per patient wave. By the mid-game (shifts 8–12), you can expect 2–3 anomalies per wave. Late-game shifts (shifts 15+) may throw **4+ anomalies** at you in a single wave.

### 2. Anomaly Variety

Not all anomaly types appear from the start. New, more dangerous anomaly types **unlock as you progress through shifts**:

| Shift Range | Anomaly Types Unlocked |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Shifts 1–3** | Basic appearance anomalies (Three Eyes, Twitching, Hollow Face) |
| **Shifts 4–7** | Photo anomalies (Cursed Photo, Static Photo, Different Eyes) |
| **Shifts 7–10** | CCTV anomalies (Void Bodies, Staring At Camera, Shadow Figure) |
| **Shifts 10–13** | Rare anomalies and advanced detection types |
| **Shifts 14+** | All anomaly types, including the rarest and most deceptive ones |

This gradual unlock means early shifts are a learning curve, while later shifts test your mastery of the full anomaly roster.

### 3. Monster Intensity

Monsters become more aggressive and frequent as shifts progress:

| Shift Range | Monster Activity |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Shifts 1–5** | Rare monster encounters, mostly Bed Monster and Head Banger |
| **Shifts 6–10** | Skinwalker appears more often; Stalker becomes persistent |
| **Shifts 11–15** | Multiple monster types active simultaneously; Hiders are common |
| **Shifts 16+** | Near-constant monster pressure; special encounters during final shift |

## Shift Flow — What Happens Each Shift

Every shift follows a consistent structure, though the specifics intensify over time:

### Phase 1: Startup (0–60 seconds)

- Players spawn in the hospital and take their positions.
- **Gather supplies** — grab Coffee from break rooms, Chocolate from supply rooms.
- **Assign roles** — decide who handles reception, CCTV, healing, and monster defense.
- First patients begin arriving at the reception window.

### Phase 2: Patient Screening (60 seconds–mid-shift)

- The bulk of gameplay happens here.
- **Screen each patient** using the 3-step protocol: Appearance → Photo → CCTV.
- **Admit or reject** based on your findings.
- **Treat admitted patients** in treatment rooms.
- **Monitor Sanity** and use items as needed.

### Phase 3: Escalation (mid-shift–2 minutes remaining)

- **Anomaly frequency increases** during this phase.
- **Monster encounters become more common**.
- **Environmental events** may trigger (lights flicker, doors slam).
- This is where most players lose Sanity — stay focused and use your items.

### Phase 4: Final Push (last 2 minutes)

- The game throws everything at you.
- **Maximum anomaly pressure**.
- **Multiple monsters may be active simultaneously**.
- **Survival is the only goal** — don't worry about perfect screening, just stay alive.
- If your Sanity is healthy, ride it out. If it's low, pop Chocolate and avoid all threats.

### Phase 5: Shift Complete (timer hits 0)

- If you're alive, the shift is **complete** and counts toward your total.
- You return to the lobby and can spend earned Animal Coins and dollars.
- The next shift will be **slightly harder** than the last.

| Shift | Milestone | What Changes |

## Survival Tips for Late Shifts

Reaching the later shifts requires a combination of **mechanical skill, game knowledge, and team coordination**. Here are the most important strategies:

### 1. Master All Three Detection Methods

By shift 10+, you need to be proficient at all three screening methods:

- **Appearance** — memorize all visual anomalies (twitching, extra eyes, hollow face, etc.).
- **Photo** — always inspect photos on the desk before picking them up to avoid Cursed Photos.
- **CCTV** — assign a dedicated CCTV watcher for the waiting room camera.

Check our [Beginner Guide](/guide/beginner-guide/) if you need to brush up on the basics.

### 2. Build a Balanced Team

Late shifts are nearly impossible solo. Build a team with clear roles:

| Role | Best Class | Primary Job |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Receptionist** | Secretary | Screen patients, admit/reject, recover Sanity |
| **CCTV Monitor** | Any | Watch waiting room camera full-time, call out anomalies |
| **Healer** | Surgeon or Doctor | Treat patients, maintain team Sanity |
| **Security** | Secret Agent or Intern+Gun | Handle Skinwalker, Hiders, and other monsters |
| **Flexible** | Psychologist | Support role, boost team Sanity with doubled item effects |

### 3. Manage Sanity Aggressively

In late shifts, Sanity drains faster than ever. You need a proactive strategy:

- **Never let Sanity drop below 30%** — below this threshold, visual distortion makes anomaly detection unreliable.
- **Stockpile Chocolate** — grab every Chocolate you find during early and mid-shift phases.
- **Use Coffee proactively** — don't wait for Sanity to drop; sip Coffee between patients to maintain a buffer.
- **Play Sanity-focused classes** — Surgeon, Doctor, and Psychologist all provide passive Sanity recovery.

See our [Sanity Management Guide](/guide/sanity-management-guide/) for a complete breakdown of Sanity mechanics.

### 4. Know When to Cut Losses

In late shifts, **survival matters more than accuracy**. If you're unsure about a patient:

- **Close the shutters** — better safe than sorry.
- **Don't spend more than 5 seconds** on a single patient — the next anomaly might already be at the window.
- **Prioritize your own Sanity** over catching every anomaly.

### 5. Learn Monster Patterns

Each monster has a counter. Knowing these counters becomes essential in late shifts:

| Monster | Counter | Priority |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Skinwalker** | Gun (20 shots) or X-Taser | Highest — it kills players |
| **Bed Monster** | Hop over bed or use Maple Syrup | High — it blocks a treatment room |
| **Hiders** | Run to lobby or Gun | High — they're unpredictable |
| **The Stalker** | Don't look at it | Medium — Sanity drain adds up |
| **Don't Look Up** | Eye Drops | Medium — persistent Sanity drain |
| **Camera Anomaly** | Press Exit immediately | Medium — -20 Sanity if missed |
| **Head Banger** | Close shutter | Low — slow and predictable |
| **Death Ritual** | Remove candles before timer | High — cumulative Sanity drain |

## FAQ — Shift Questions

### Does dying reset your shift progress?

**No.** Dying eliminates you from the current shift (it doesn't count), but your overall shift progress is **never reset**. You keep all your completed shifts and continue from where you left off.

### Can you replay earlier shifts?

Animal Hospital does not have a shift-select menu. You always progress to the next shift after completing one. However, joining a new server or playing with a party may place you at an earlier shift in the progression.

### Do all servers have the same shift count?

Shift progression appears to be **account-based** rather than server-based. Your progress carries across servers. The total shift count should be consistent regardless of which server you join.

### Is the shift timer the same every time?

The base shift duration is consistent, but certain events and anomalies can make shifts **feel** longer or shorter. The actual timer is fixed.
