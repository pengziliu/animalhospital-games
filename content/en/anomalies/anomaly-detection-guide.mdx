# Anomaly Detection Guide – 3-Step Screening Protocol

Every patient in Animal Hospital could be normal — or could be harboring an anomaly. The **3-step screening protocol** is the standard method for catching every type of anomaly reliably. Follow these steps in order for every single patient, and you'll catch anomalies before they can cause harm.

---

## Why This Order Matters

| Step | Anomaly Types Covered | Why It's First |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Appearance** | Twitching, Three Eyes, Hollow Face, Wide Eyes, Creepy Smile, Human Teeth, Mismatched Eyes | Sets a **mental baseline** for Steps 2 and 3. You need to know what the patient actually looks like. |
| **2. Photo** | Different Eyes, Different Ears, Incorrect Photo, Unnatural Photo, Static Photo, Cursed Photo | Uses the **baseline from Step 1** to detect discrepancies. Also avoids Cursed Photo Sanity drain. |
| **3. CCTV** | Censored Eyes, Unnatural Body, Hollow Face CCTV, Staring At Camera, Void Bodies, Skinwalker, Mismatched CCTV, Twitching CCTV | **Final safety net.** Some anomalies only appear on cameras. The waiting room camera is the priority. |

## Step 1: Appearance Check — Deep Dive

### What to Look For

| Priority | Anomaly | How to Spot | Time Needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Three Eyes | Red eye on forehead | 1 second |
| 2 | Hollow Face | Black void eye sockets | 1 second |
| 3 | Wide Eyes / Creepy Smile | Distorted facial proportions | 1 second |
| 4 | Human Teeth | Wrong teeth for species | 2 seconds |
| 5 | Twitching | Limb jerking (watch 4–5s) | 5 seconds |
| 6 | Mismatched Eyes & Sharp Teeth | Asymmetric eyes + fangs | 3 seconds |

### Best Practices

- **Always face the patient directly.** Side glances are unreliable.
- **Scan top-to-bottom:** Face first (eyes, mouth), then body (twitching).
- **Take your time on Medium anomalies.** 5 seconds of observation for Twitching is worth the investment.

### Common Mistakes

- ❌ Glancing at the patient while walking past (misses Twitching and Mismatched Eyes)
- ❌ Skipping this step entirely (breaks the baseline needed for Steps 2 and 3)

## Step 2: Photo Check — Deep Dive

### What to Look For

| Priority | Anomaly | How to Spot | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Static Photo | TV-static covering the image | None (Easy) |
| 2 | Incorrect Photo | Different patient/scene entirely | None (Easy) |
| 3 | Unnatural Photo | Warped or distorted image | None (Easy) |
| 4 | Cursed Photo | Bloodshot, glowing eyes | **-10 Sanity if touched** |
| 5 | Different Ears | Ears don't match in-person | None (Medium) |
| 6 | Different Eyes | Eyes don't match in-person | None (Hard) |

### Best Practices

- **Look at the photo on the desk BEFORE picking it up.** This prevents Cursed Photo Sanity drain.
- **Compare specific features:** Match the photo's eyes and ears against your mental snapshot from Step 1.
- **If in doubt, close the shutter.** It's better to dismiss a clean patient than to let an anomaly through.

### Common Mistakes

- ❌ Picking up the photo without looking first (Cursed Photo trap)
- ❌ Not remembering the patient's appearance from Step 1 (can't detect Different Eyes/Ears)

---

## Step 3: CCTV Check — Deep Dive

### What to Look For

| Priority | Anomaly | How to Spot | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Void Bodies | Dark shadow figure | Easy |
| 2 | Censored/JPEG Eyes | Blurred eye area | Easy |
| 3 | Unnatural Body | Stretched limbs | Easy |
| 4 | Staring At Camera | Patient looks at lens | Medium |
| 5 | Skinwalker | Erratic movement | Medium |
| 6 | Twitching | Limb jerking on feed | Hard |
| 7 | Hollow Face | Empty sockets on CCTV | Hard |
| 8 | Mismatched Ears/Eyes | Differs from in-person | Very Hard |

### Best Practices

- **Start with the waiting room camera.** It's the most important feed and where most CCTV anomalies appear.
- **Watch each patient for a few seconds** before moving on.
- **Cross-reference with Step 1 memory** for the Mismatched anomaly.

### Common Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring CCTV entirely (misses anomalies exclusive to cameras)
- ❌ Rushing past the Very Hard anomaly (Mismatched Ears/Eyes requires deliberate effort)

## Advanced Tips

### Speed Optimization

With practice, you can complete the full 3-step protocol in under 30 seconds per patient:

- **Step 1:** ~10 seconds (quick face scan + 5s twitching observation)
- **Step 2:** ~8 seconds (desk inspection + comparison)
- **Step 3:** ~10 seconds (CCTV scan starting with waiting room)

### Sanity Preservation During Screening

- Don't pick up photos you haven't visually inspected.
- Don't look at The Stalker if it appears during your screening.
- Keep Coffee or Chocolate handy for Sanity recovery between patients.

### Multi-Anomaly Situations

Some patients can have **anomalies across multiple categories** (e.g., a normal appearance but a Cursed Photo AND a CCTV anomaly). The 3-step protocol catches all of them because each step checks a different detection channel.
