# All Anomalies in Animal Hospital

Anomalies are disguised monsters mixed in with regular animal patients. They can be identified by appearance, photo anomalies, CCTV footage, or by encountering monsters roaming the hospital. There are three main identification methods: Appearance (visual inspection), Photo (check polaroid), and CCTV (check surveillance cameras).

## Detection Methods

There are **three main ways** to identify anomalies disguised as patients:

1. **Appearance** — Look directly at the patient at the reception desk
2. **Photo** — Take a polaroid and wait for it to develop on the desk
3. **CCTV** — Check surveillance cameras, especially the waiting room camera

## Anomaly Screening Checklist

Before admitting any patient, follow this checklist:

- [ ] Look at the patient directly for 4-5 seconds
- [ ] Take a photo and wait for it to develop on the desk
- [ ] Check the waiting room CCTV camera
- [ ] Compare the photo to the patient appearance
- [ ] If ANYTHING seems off, close the shutters immediately

## Pro Tips

- The **waiting room CCTV** is the most important camera — check it for every patient
- **Cursed photos** drain 10 Sanity if picked up — close the shutter first
- Some anomalies only appear on **one detection method** — always use all three
- **Audio cues** (Skinwalker roars, Hider breathing) can alert you to danger
- Keep cameras in good repair — broken cameras leave you blind
