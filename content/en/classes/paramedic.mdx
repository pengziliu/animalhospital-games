# Paramedic — C Tier Class

The Paramedic trades raw healing power for mobility, starting each round with a Large Speed Cola that lets you traverse the hospital faster. Speed helps with response times, but it does not stop Sanity drain or death — limiting the class's overall effectiveness.

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Cost** | 250 Animal Coins |
| **Tier** | C |
| **Role** | Mobile Support / Response |
| **Rarity** | Standard |
| Sanity | None (Level 1–2), Low (Level 3 regen) |
| Utility | Medium |

## Core Ability

Start each round with Large Speed Cola (6 uses) — a consumable that significantly boosts your movement speed for a limited duration, allowing faster patient response and map traversal.

## Upgrade Levels

### Level 1
Start with Large Speed Cola (6 uses)

### Level 2
Start with Large Speed Cola (9 uses)

### Level 3
Start with Large Speed Cola (9 uses), +1 Speed Cola regenerates each round

## Strategy Tips

Movement speed in Animal Hospital helps you reach patients faster and cover more ground during emergencies. The Paramedic's Large Speed Cola gives you six speed boosts per round at Level 1, which is meaningful if you play a highly mobile support role — rushing between rooms, fetching supplies from distant storage, or evacuating patients. At Level 3, the per-round regeneration means you effectively never run out of speed boosts, making sustained fast play viable.

However, speed alone does not solve the game's core challenges. It does not recover Sanity, heal patients, or neutralize threats. A fast player who arrives at a patient's room with low Sanity is still at risk of breakdown. The Paramedic is best paired with a team that handles healing while you focus on logistics and rapid response. As a solo class, its limitations are stark — consider it a support role enhancer rather than a standalone powerhouse.

## Comparison to Adjacent Tier Classes

| Class | Cost | Key Benefit |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **Nurse** | 10 Coins | +1 Inventory Slot |
| **Paramedic** | 250 Coins | Large Speed Cola (6 uses) |
| **Doctor** | 900 Coins | Sanity on Heal |

The Paramedic costs 25 times more than the Nurse for a fundamentally different utility — speed versus inventory. Whether that trade-off is worth it depends on your playstyle. The Doctor, at 900 coins, costs 3.6 times more but provides self-sustaining Sanity recovery that has a much larger impact on survival and performance. For most players, saving the 250 coins toward a Doctor unlock is the smarter path. The Paramedic is a fun and flashy class, but it struggles to compete with the raw utility of higher-tier options.
