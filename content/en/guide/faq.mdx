# Animal Hospital FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Animal Hospital on Roblox, answered. From basic questions for new players to advanced mechanics for veterans.

## General Questions

### What is Animal Hospital?

**Animal Hospital** (also known as Animal Hospital: Anomaly) is a **co-op survival horror game** on Roblox where you work the night shift at a veterinary clinic. Your job is to screen animal patients for hidden anomalies — monsters disguised as ordinary animals — while maintaining your Sanity and surviving the shift.

- **Developer**: Animal Anomaly (led by Roytt)
- **Release Date**: May 10, 2026
- **Genre**: Co-op survival horror
- **Total Visits**: 45.2M+
- **Peak CCU**: 135,000+
- **Rating**: 95.8% positive

### Is Animal Hospital free to play?

**Yes, 100% free.** There are no paywalls or premium entry requirements. Anyone with a Roblox account can play immediately. Optional purchases (Robux classes and items) enhance gameplay but are not required to enjoy the full experience.

### How many players can play together?

Up to **30 players** per server. The game supports both solo play and full co-op teams. Smaller groups of 3–6 players tend to perform best due to easier coordination.

### Is Animal Hospital on mobile?

**Yes.** Animal Hospital is available on mobile devices through the Roblox app. However, the game is best experienced on PC due to the precise camera movements and quick reaction times needed for anomaly detection. Mobile players can still participate fully in all game modes.

### What age rating does Animal Hospital have?

Animal Hospital contains horror elements including jumpscares, supernatural themes, and monster encounters. It is appropriate for most Roblox players but may be frightening for younger children.

## Gameplay Questions

### What are anomalies?

**Anomalies** are monsters disguised as normal animal patients. They blend in with regular patients and can only be identified through three detection methods:

1. **Appearance** — Look at the patient directly at reception (e.g., Three Eyes, Hollow Face, Twitching)
2. **Photo** — Check the polaroid that develops on your desk (e.g., Static Photo, Cursed Photo, Different Eyes)
3. **CCTV** — Monitor surveillance cameras (e.g., Void Bodies, Skinwalker, Staring At Camera)

If you let an anomaly through, it drains your team's Sanity and can trigger dangerous events.

### How does Sanity work?

**Sanity is your health bar.** If it reaches 0, you're eliminated from the shift.

| What Drains Sanity | Amount |
|--------------------|--------|
| Cursed Photo (picking it up) | -10 |
| Camera Anomaly / Demon Cat (jumpscare) | -20 |
| The Stalker (eye contact) | -10 per stare |
| Unchecked anomalies | Varies |
| Don't Look Up (looking at ceiling) | Continuous drain |
| General exposure (over time) | Slow drain |

| What Restores Sanity | Amount |
|---------------------|--------|
| Coffee (per sip) | +5 (3 sips = 15 total) |
| Chocolate (per bite) | +20 (3 bites = 60 total) |
| Doctor / Surgeon healing | Varies by level |
| Secretary check-in | Varies by level |
| Psychologist class | 2× all Sanity effects |

### What happens at 0 Sanity?

When your Sanity reaches **0**, you are **eliminated from the current shift**. You can no longer participate until the next shift begins. This is effectively "death" — losing one team member makes anomaly screening harder for everyone else.

### How do you deal with the Skinwalker?

The **Skinwalker** is one of the most dangerous monsters in the game. Here's how to handle it:

| Method | How It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| **Gun** | Shoot it — 20 shots available, most reliable method |
| **Taser** | Stun it with the X-Taser (5 uses, free at Reception Office) |
| **Hold E** | Forcefully dislodge the Skinwalker from its victim |
| **Spam E** | Punch the Skinwalker if it attacks you directly |
| **Beat the timer** | Complete the encounter before the timer runs out — otherwise it eats you or the patient |

The Secret Agent class is the best counter to Skinwalkers due to the built-in Gun.

### What is the Stalker?

The **Stalker** is a monster that stares at you from around the hospital. If you make **eye contact**, you lose **10 Sanity** per stare. The best strategy is to simply **look away** — it's not lethal, but the Sanity drain adds up over a shift.

## Classes & Progression

### What is the best class?

| Category | Best Class | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Best Overall (Free)** | **Surgeon** (2,500 AC) | Every heal restores Sanity + grants speed boost. Level 3 doubles Sanity gain. |
| **Best Overall (Paid)** | **Secret Agent** (320–890 R$) | Built-in Gun with 20 shots. Level 3 regenerates shots. |
| **Best Budget** | **Doctor** (900 AC) | Sanity recovery on healing. Affordable and effective. |
| **Best Support** | **Psychologist** (500 AC) | 2× Sanity effects — doubles the value of Coffee and Chocolate. |
| **Best Starter** | **Intern** (Free) | +10 Bonus Sanity. No cost, decent buffer for beginners. |

### How do you unlock classes?

Classes are purchased in the **lobby Class Shop** using:

- **Animal Coins** (earned in-game by completing shifts)
- **Robux** (premium Roblox currency, real money)

Each class has **3 upgrade levels** with progressively stronger effects.

### What is the recommended class progression?

```
Intern (Free) → Doctor (900 AC) → Surgeon (2,500 AC) → [Optional] Secret Agent (Robux)
```

This path takes you from a basic free class to the most powerful earnable class, then optionally adds the best paid combat class.

## Economy & Currency

### How do you get Animal Coins?

Animal Coins are earned by **completing shifts**. The amount varies based on:

- **Class used** — Higher-tier classes tend to earn more
- **Shift length** — Longer shifts = more coins
- **Efficiency** — More patients treated = higher rewards
- **Anomalies caught** — Successfully identifying anomalies contributes to your score

Most players earn **50–200 Animal Coins per shift** depending on class and performance.

## Advanced Questions

### Can you play solo?

**Yes.** Animal Hospital is fully playable solo. The best solo class is **Surgeon** for self-sustaining Sanity recovery. The main challenges of solo play are:

- You handle all screening alone (no parallel CCTV watching)
- Monster encounters (Skinwalker, Hiders) are more dangerous without team support
- Patient throughput is slower with only one player

### What's the difference between Animal Coins and dollars (in-game cash)?

| Currency | Earned By | Used For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Animal Coins (AC)** | Completing shifts | Buying permanent classes |
| **In-Game Dollars ($)** | Treating patients, shift rewards | Buying upgrades and consumable items |

Animal Coins are the **premium progression currency** — save them for classes. Dollars are **shift-to-shift currency** — spend freely on upgrades and items.

### How do I improve at anomaly detection?

1. **Follow the 3-step protocol every time** — Appearance → Photo → CCTV
2. **Spend 4–5 seconds on the appearance check** — don't rush the twitching test
3. **Always inspect photos on the desk before picking up** — prevents Cursed Photo damage
4. **Check the waiting room CCTV for every patient** — it's the most important camera
5. **Memorize the anomaly types** — use our [Anomaly Cheat Sheet](/guide/how-to-identify-anomalies)

### What are the monsters and hazards?
