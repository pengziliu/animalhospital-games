# Intern — D Tier Class

The default starting class everyone begins with. While its stats are the lowest in the game, the passive Sanity bonus provides a small cushion during your first few shifts while you learn the ropes and save up for better classes.

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Cost** | Free (Default Starter) |
| **Tier** | D |
| **Role** | General / Learning |
| **Rarity** | Default |
| Sanity | Low-Medium |
| Utility | None |

## Core Ability

+10 Bonus Sanity — starts each shift with 10 extra Sanity points beyond the base value, giving you a slightly longer window before hitting critical Sanity levels.

## Upgrade Levels

### Level 1
+10 Bonus Sanity

### Level 2
+15 Bonus Sanity

### Level 3
+20 Bonus Sanity

## Strategy Tips

As the starter class, the Intern is designed to get you through your first few games without immediately burning out on Sanity. The +10 passive means you can afford a couple extra mistakes or take on one more patient before needing a break. Prioritize learning the hospital layout, check-in flow, and which upgrades to save for.

Upgrade the Intern to Level 3 only if you are committed to grinding Animal Coins without spending them on a new class. The Level 3 +20 Sanity buffer is decent but still outclassed by the Doctor's on-heal Sanity recovery at 900 coins, which is self-sustaining rather than a one-time buffer. Use the Intern until you can afford at least the Nurse (10 coins) or ideally the Doctor, then switch immediately.

## Comparison to Adjacent Tier Classes

| Class | Cost | Key Benefit |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **Intern** | Free | +10 Bonus Sanity (starter) |
| **Nurse** | 10 Coins | +1 Inventory Slot |
| **Doctor** | 900 Coins | Sanity on Heal |

The Intern's only real advantage is being free. Even the Nurse at just 10 coins offers an inventory upgrade, and the Doctor at 900 coins introduces self-sustaining Sanity recovery that makes the Intern's flat bonus look trivial. Treat the Intern as a tutorial class — learn the basics, then graduate.
