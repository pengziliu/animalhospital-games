# How to Identify Anomalies — Complete Detection Guide

Identifying anomalies quickly and accurately is the core skill in Animal Hospital. Every animal patient arriving at your clinic could be a disguised monster. This guide covers the **three detection methods**, detailed visual descriptions of every anomaly, difficulty ratings, and a cheat sheet you can reference mid-game.

---

## The 3 Detection Methods

Anomalies can only be identified through three specific channels. Some anomalies appear on only one channel, while others span multiple. **You must use all three methods on every patient** to guarantee detection.

| Method | Where to Check | What It Reveals | Time Needed |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Appearance** | Patient at reception desk | Visible physical deformities | ~10 seconds |
| **Photo (Polaroid)** | Photo develops on your desk | Hidden discrepancies, cursed images | ~8 seconds |
| **CCTV** | Surveillance monitors (waiting room priority) | Camera-only anomalies | ~10 seconds |

---

## Method 1: Appearance Anomalies

These anomalies are visible when you **look directly at the patient** at the reception window. No camera or photo needed — your eyes are the only tool.

### Easy — Instantly Recognizable

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like | How to Spot |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Three Eyes** | A patient with a bright **red third eye** on its forehead | Glance at the face — the red eye is immediately obvious |
| **Hollow Face** | The patient's face has **black void eye sockets** — no eyes, no features, just empty darkness | The complete absence of facial features makes this one of the easiest to spot |
| **Wide Eyes & Creepy Smile** | The patient has **distorted facial proportions** — unnaturally wide eyes stretched open with an unsettling, too-wide grin | Look at the face — the proportions are wrong in an obvious way |
| **Human Teeth** | The animal has **human-like teeth** instead of species-appropriate teeth | Check the mouth — teeth that belong on a person, not on an animal |

### Medium — Requires a Closer Look

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like | How to Spot |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Twitching / Limb Spasms** | The patient's limbs **jerk and spasm involuntarily** — legs or arms twitch in an unnatural, mechanical way | Watch the patient for **4–5 seconds**. The twitching is subtle and may not be visible in a quick glance |
| **Mismatched Eyes & Sharp Teeth (Poorly Drawn Patient)** | The patient has **asymmetric eyes** (different sizes or colors) paired with **fang-like sharp teeth** | Compare both eyes to each other, then check the mouth. The asymmetry is the giveaway |

### Tips for Appearance Checks

- ✅ **Face the patient directly** — side glances are unreliable
- ✅ **Scan top-to-bottom**: Eyes → Mouth → Body (for twitching)
- ✅ **Spend the full 4–5 seconds** watching for twitching before moving on
- ❌ Never skip this step — it sets the **mental baseline** needed for Photo and CCTV comparisons

## Method 2: Photo Anomalies

Photo anomalies are **invisible in person**. They only appear when the **polaroid develops on your desk**. Some photo anomalies are dangerous to touch.

### Easy — Obvious Photo Problems

| Anomaly | What the Photo Looks Like | Risk |
|---------|--------------------------|------|
| **Static Photo** | The entire polaroid is covered in **TV-static interference** — white and gray noise across the image | None — purely visual |
| **Incorrect Photo** | The polaroid shows a **completely different patient or scene** — not the animal you just saw | None — obvious mismatch |
| **Unnatural Photo** | The image is **warped, stretched, or distorted** — like a funhouse mirror version of the patient | None — visually obvious |

### Medium — Needs Comparison with Step 1

| Anomaly | What the Photo Looks Like | Risk |
|---------|--------------------------|------|
| **Different Ears** | The ears in the photo **don't match** what you saw in-person — wrong shape, size, or position | None — must compare against your Appearance check memory |
| **Cursed Photo** | The photo has **bloodshot, glowing eyes** and an eerie aura. Picking it up drains **10 Sanity** | **-10 Sanity** if you touch it — close the shutter BEFORE picking it up |

### Hard — Subtle Differences

| Anomaly | What the Photo Looks Like | Risk |
|---------|--------------------------|------|
| **Different Eyes** | The eyes in the photo **don't match** the in-person patient — slightly different color, shape, or number | None — requires close comparison with your mental snapshot from Step 1 |

### Tips for Photo Checks

- ✅ **Look at the photo on the desk BEFORE picking it up** — this prevents Cursed Photo Sanity drain
- ✅ **Compare specific features**: Match eyes and ears against what you saw in Step 1
- ✅ **If in doubt, close the shutter** — dismissing a clean patient is always safer than letting an anomaly through
- ❌ Never grab the photo without inspecting it first

## Method 3: CCTV Anomalies

CCTV anomalies are **invisible both in-person AND in photos**. They only appear on **surveillance camera feeds**. The **waiting room camera** is the most important — check it first.

### Easy — Visible Anomalies

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like on Camera | Difficulty |
|---------|------------------------------|-----------|
| **Void Bodies / Shadow Patient** | A **dark, featureless shadow figure** in place of where the patient should be on the feed | Easy — a black silhouette stands out immediately |
| **Censored Eyes / JPEG Eyes** | The patient's eye area is **blurred or pixelated** like a censored photograph | Easy — the blur is immediately noticeable on the feed |
| **Unnatural Body / Limb Stretching** | The patient's body on camera shows **stretched, elongated limbs** that don't match normal proportions | Easy — limbs that are too long or bent wrong |

### Medium — Requires Attention

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like on Camera | Difficulty |
|---------|------------------------------|-----------|
| **Staring At Camera** | The patient on the feed is **looking directly into the camera lens** — normal patients don't do this | Medium — the direct stare is unsettling but not instantly alarming |
| **Skinwalker (CCTV)** | The patient displays **erratic, unnatural movement** on the camera feed — jerky, fast, or wrong-direction movement | Medium — the movement pattern is the giveaway |

### Hard — Very Subtle

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like on Camera | Difficulty |
|---------|------------------------------|-----------|
| **Twitching (CCTV)** | The patient's limbs **jerk on the camera feed** — same as the appearance version but only visible on monitors | Hard — easy to miss on a small screen with other patients in frame |
| **Hollow Face (CCTV)** | The patient's face on the camera shows **empty black void eye sockets** | Hard — depends on camera angle and feed quality |

### Very Hard — Expert Level

| Anomaly | What It Looks Like on Camera | Difficulty |
|---------|------------------------------|-----------|
| **Mismatched Ears or Eyes (CCTV)** | The patient on CCTV has **different ears or eyes** compared to what you saw in-person during Step 1 | Very Hard — requires strong memory of the patient's in-person appearance and deliberate cross-referencing |

### Tips for CCTV Checks

- ✅ **Start with the waiting room camera** — most CCTV anomalies appear there
- ✅ **Watch each patient for a few seconds** before cycling to the next camera
- ✅ **Cross-reference with your Step 1 memory** for the Mismatched anomaly
- ❌ Don't ignore CCTV — these anomalies are completely invisible through the other two methods

## Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet

Print this or keep it open on a second screen. Use it during shifts.

### Step-by-Step Protocol

```
1. LOOK at patient (eyes → mouth → body, 4–5 seconds)
   → Anything weird? CLOSE SHUTTER.

2. CHECK photo on desk (DON'T pick up yet)
   → Static/Wrong/Unnatural/Cursed? CLOSE SHUTTER.
   → Eyes/Ears match what you saw? Continue.

3. CHECK waiting room CCTV first, then other cameras
   → Shadow/Blur/Stretch/Staring/Jerky? CLOSE SHUTTER.
   → Everything matches? Patient is CLEAN ✓
```

### Red Flags at a Glance

| Red Flag | Method | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Red eye on forehead | Appearance | Close shutter |
| Black void face | Appearance / CCTV | Close shutter |
| Creepy wide smile | Appearance | Close shutter |
| Human teeth | Appearance | Close shutter |
| Jerking limbs | Appearance / CCTV | Watch 5 seconds, then decide |
| TV static on photo | Photo | Close shutter |
| Wrong patient in photo | Photo | Close shutter |
| Glowing eyes on photo | Photo | Close shutter (DON'T touch!) |
| Shadow figure on camera | CCTV | Close shutter |
| Pixelated eyes on camera | CCTV | Close shutter |
| Staring into camera | CCTV | Close shutter |
| Photo/CCTV doesn't match in-person | Photo / CCTV | Close shutter |

### Sanity Warnings

- 🚫 **Cursed Photo**: -10 Sanity if picked up — inspect on desk first
- 🚫 **The Stalker**: -10 Sanity per eye contact — look away
- 🚫 **Don't Look Up**: Continuous drain — use Eye Drops immediately

## Advanced: Multi-Channel Anomalies

Some patients can have anomalies across **multiple detection channels** simultaneously. For example, a patient might look normal in-person but have BOTH a Cursed Photo AND a Void Body on CCTV. This is why the **3-step protocol is mandatory** — skipping any step can let a multi-channel anomaly slip through.

> 💡 **Pro Tip**: If you spot an anomaly on ANY step, close the shutter immediately. Don't bother checking the remaining steps — the patient is already flagged.
