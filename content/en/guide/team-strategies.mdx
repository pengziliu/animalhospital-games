# Team Strategies — Best Co-op Class Combos & Roles

Animal Hospital supports up to **30 players per server**, and coordinating your team can be the difference between a clean shift and total chaos. This guide covers optimal class compositions, role assignments, communication tactics, solo strategies, and how to survive when late-shift events overwhelm uncoordinated teams.

---

## Why Team Composition Matters

With 10 available classes, each filling a different role, a balanced team dramatically outperforms a group of random classes. The right combo means:

- **Faster patient throughput** — more patients screened and treated per shift
- **Better Sanity sustain** — healers keep the team alive
- **Reliable anomaly elimination** — armed players handle monsters quickly
- **Full CCTV coverage** — no blind spots in surveillance

---

## Best Class Combos

### 🥇 Best Overall: Surgeon + Secret Agent + Head Nurse

This trio covers every essential role: healing, combat, and inventory management.

| Player | Class | Role | Why |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| Player 1 | **Surgeon** (2,500 AC) | Healer / Medic | Best self-sustaining class. Every heal restores Sanity + grants speed boost. Level 3 doubles Sanity gain — nearly unkillable. |
| Player 2 | **Secret Agent** (320–890 R$) | Combat / Security | Starts with a Gun (20 shots) for eliminating Skinwalkers, Hiders, and other roaming monsters. Level 3 regenerates 1 shot per round. |
| Player 3 | **Head Nurse** (80–190 R$) | Support / Logistics | +3 Max Inventory means carrying Chocolate, Coffee, Eye Drops, Maple Syrup, and a Gun simultaneously — the ultimate utility player. |

**Why this combo works**: The Surgeon keeps everyone alive, the Secret Agent handles any monster threat instantly, and the Head Nurse distributes Sanity items and utility tools across the hospital.

## Role Assignment Guide

Every successful team needs clear role assignments. Here are the **six core roles** and which classes fit each:

### 1. Receptionist (Anomaly Screener)

**Goal**: Screen every patient at the front desk using the 3-step protocol.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Secretary | Sanity on check-in — rewards you for doing the screening job |
| Intern (free) | Good enough for beginners; the +10 Bonus Sanity gives a buffer |

**Duties**: Follow the 3-step protocol (Appearance → Photo → CCTV), close shutters on anomalies, check in clean patients.

### 2. Healer (Medic)

**Goal**: Keep team and patient Sanity above zero.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Surgeon | Best healer — Sanity + speed on every heal. Self-sustaining. |
| Doctor | Solid healer — Sanity on healing. More affordable than Surgeon. |
| Psychologist | 2x Sanity effects — makes every Coffee sip and Chocolate bite count double. |

**Duties**: Heal patients, heal teammates, distribute Sanity items during emergencies.

### 3. Monster Hunter (Security)

**Goal**: Eliminate roaming threats before they drain team Sanity or kill patients.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Secret Agent | Gun with 20 shots — highest damage, longest range. |
| Security | X-Taser (5 uses) — non-lethal but effective for stunning threats. |

**Duties**: Patrol corridors, respond to Skinwalker calls, eliminate Hiders, guard patient rooms.

### 4. Supply Runner (Logistics)

**Goal**: Ensure the team always has Sanity items and utility tools when needed.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Head Nurse | +3 Max Inventory = carry more items than anyone else. |
| Nurse | +1 Max Inventory — budget alternative. |

**Duties**: Collect Chocolate, Coffee, Eye Drops, Maple Syrup, and distribute them to players in need.

### 5. Scout (Reconnaissance)

**Goal**: Monitor CCTV cameras and alert the team to anomalies and threats.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Paramedic | Speed Cola lets you move between camera banks and the reception area quickly. |
| Intern | Budget option — just needs to sit at monitors. |

**Duties**: Watch waiting room CCTV primarily, call out CCTV anomalies, track Stalker movement.

### 6. Flex / All-Rounder

**Goal**: Fill gaps and respond to emergencies wherever they happen.

| Best Classes | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Psychologist | 2x Sanity effects make them effective in any situation. |
| Nurse | +1 inventory slot with no specialized role — pure flexibility. |

## Communication Tips

Voice chat and the in-game text chat are your most powerful tools. Use these callouts:

### Standard Callouts

| Situation | What to Say |
|-----------|-------------|
| Spotting an anomaly | "Anomaly at window — [Appearance/Photo/CCTV]" |
| Monster sighting | "Skinwalker in hallway — need gun!" |
| Low Sanity warning | "I'm at 20 Sanity — need Chocolate" |
| Item available | "Chocolate on counter near room 3" |
| Camera down | "Waiting room camera is broken — someone fix it" |
| Needing healer | "Patient dying in room 2 — need Surgeon" |

### Communication Rules

1. **One reception caller** — avoid 3 people shouting about the same patient
2. **Use class names in callouts** — "Surgeon to room 4" is faster than "the healing person come here"
3. **Call out Sanity levels proactively** — don't wait until you're at 5
4. **Confirm kills** — "Skinwalker down" lets the team stop worrying

## Solo vs Team Strategies

### Playing Solo

Playing solo is entirely possible and can be efficient if you choose the right class.

| Strategy | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Best Solo Class** | **Surgeon** — self-sustaining Sanity recovery means no dependency on teammates |
| **Budget Solo Class** | **Doctor** — 900 AC, Sanity on healing, good enough for most shifts |
| **Playstyle** | Screen patients yourself (3-step protocol), heal patients for Sanity, grab free Coffee between patients |
| **Key Items** | Chocolate (60 Sanity total), Coffee (free), Gun ($410) for emergencies |
| **Weakness** | Skinwalker encounters are dangerous without a team. Keep a Gun or Taser ready. |

### Playing in a Team

| Advantage | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Parallel screening** | One player checks CCTV while another screens at reception |
| **Distributed healing** | Multiple healers cover more patients simultaneously |
| **Monster response** | Armed players can eliminate threats while others keep screening |
| **Item sharing** | Supply runners ensure no one runs out of Sanity recovery |
| **Sanity safety net** | If one player drops to 0, others continue the shift |

## Handling Late-Shift Chaos

The end of a shift is when everything escalates: more anomalies, multiple monsters roaming, and players running low on Sanity. Here's how to survive:

### Preparation (Shift Start)

- Stock up on **Chocolate** and **Coffee** at the shop
- Ensure at least one player has a **Gun** or **Taser**
- Assign a dedicated **CCTV watcher** from the start
- Place **Fire Extinguishers** near high-risk areas

### Mid-Shift (Stable Phase)

- Maintain the 3-step screening protocol — don't get sloppy
- Healers should top off team Sanity proactively
- Supply runners should redistribute items before the chaos phase

### Late Shift (Chaos Phase)

| Threat | Response |
|--------|----------|
| **Multiple anomalies at once** | Everyone focuses on their assigned role — don't double-up on screening |
| **Skinwalker + Hider simultaneously** | Secret Agent handles Skinwalker; Taser user handles Hider; others keep screening |
| **Team low on Sanity** | Psychologist uses 2x Chocolate; Surgeon heals everyone; Head Nurse distributes remaining items |
| **Camera broken during anomaly wave** | Fall back on Appearance + Photo only — don't waste time repairing during active threats |
| **Fire outbreak** | Drop everything, grab Fire Extinguisher — fire spreads fast and damages patients |
| **Don't Look Up active** | One player with Eye Drops handles it immediately; others look down and keep working |

### Emergency Priorities

```
1. SURVIVE — Keep your Sanity above 0. Use items.
2. PROTECT PATIENTS — Heal dying patients to prevent deaths.
3. ELIMINATE THREATS — Gun/Taser active monsters.
4. SCREEN PATIENTS — Resume normal operations once safe.
```
